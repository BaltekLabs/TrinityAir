⚡ Breaking News — March 9, 2026: U.S. Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy announced Texas DOT + Archer Aviation as one of 8 national selections for the White House eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP). Named routes connect Dallas, Austin, San Antonio, and Houston. Archer Midnight commercial air taxi operations begin in Texas this summer. Fort Worth is not yet on the route map. This brief explains why that must change — and how the Trinity Air Link makes it happen.

The Opportunity for Fort Worth

Texas is now one of only eight states in the nation with a federally sanctioned electric air taxi program. The federal government has designated this state — and its partner, Archer Aviation — to prove that commercial air taxi operations can work at scale. Routes are already announced for Dallas, Austin, San Antonio, and Houston.

Fort Worth — Texas's second million-plus city, the fastest-growing city in the DFW corridor, and a municipality with an iconic transportation landmark sitting vacant downtown — has no designated vertiport. The Trinity Air Link corrects that.

What the Link Proposes

The Trinity Air Link would transform the historic Texas & Pacific Warehouse — vacant since 1998 — into America's first integrated eVTOL and autonomous vehicle transportation hub:

  • Rooftop vertiport: Multiple simultaneous aircraft landing pads with charging systems and a passenger terminal
  • Ground-level AV terminal: Level 4 autonomous vehicle fleet for first- and last-mile connectivity across Fort Worth
  • 120,000 sq ft Innovation Center: Floors 2–7 leased to transportation technology companies, research institutions, and startups
  • Public showcase floor: Community education center, STEM programs, and interactive technology exhibits

The Texas & Pacific Warehouse — 8 stories, 480,000 sq ft, vacant since 1998.

Public-Private Partnership Structure

The city's financial risk is zero. 100% of the $129M capital investment is raised from private sources. The City's role is regulatory facilitation, infrastructure coordination, and community engagement — the same role it plays in any large-scale development.

What the City Provides

  • Regulatory facilitation: Streamlined permitting and zoning support
  • eIPP endorsement letter: Letter to TxDOT and U.S. DOT supporting Fort Worth vertiport designation
  • Infrastructure coordination: Utility connections and traffic management integration
  • Historic preservation: Collaboration with Landmarks Commission
  • Community engagement: Joint public education and outreach
  • Economic development: Tax incentive programs (existing frameworks)

What the City Receives

  • $25M+ in annual tax revenue by Year 5
  • 3,300+ jobs created with no municipal capital at risk
  • National recognition as an eIPP hub city
  • T&P Warehouse resolved after 27 years of vacancy and code violations
  • Transportation relief — measurable reduction in Fort Worth's 10th-worst commute
