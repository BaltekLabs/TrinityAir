T&P Nexus HUB Transportation System Executive Summary · March 2026

The Vision: Fort Worth's Transportation Future Starts Here

The T&P Nexus HUB is a proposed public-private transformation of the historic Texas & Pacific Warehouse in downtown Fort Worth into America's first fully integrated multi-modal transportation hub — combining commercial eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing) aircraft, Level 4 autonomous vehicles, a 120,000 SF innovation center, and a restored historic landmark into a single, seamlessly connected facility.


Fort Worth is the 12th largest city in the United States, the fastest-growing large city in Texas, and faces a transportation crisis of national significance. The HUB addresses that crisis with proven technology, a phased capital plan, and a model replicable across every major U.S. metro.

eVTOL Operations (Rooftop)

Landing pads + charging infrastructure for commercial eVTOL aircraft. Partners include Archer Aviation (Texas eIPP-selected, Part 135 certified, commercial 2026) and Joby Aviation (FAA Stage 4, Toyota-backed $1B+). Point-to-point fares: $75–$150/trip at 150–200 mph.

Autonomous Vehicle Hub (Ground)

Level 4 AV terminal for first/last mile connectivity across DFW. Waymo operates commercially in Austin TX (March 2025) with 450K+ rides/week. AV platform brings 88% fewer property damage claims, 92% fewer injury claims vs. human drivers (Swiss Re, 25.3M miles).

Innovation Center (Floors 2–7)

120,000 SF of Class A technology workspace in a nationally recognized historic building. Fort Worth Class A rents: $30.68/SF (CommercialCafe, 2025). Tenants: transportation tech, aerospace, AI, and logistics companies requiring proximity to operating eVTOL/AV infrastructure.

The Asset: T&P Warehouse

The 8-story Texas & Pacific Warehouse (600 ft × 100 ft) is an architecturally significant historic property adjacent to Fort Worth's Amtrak station, TEXRail terminus, and Trinity Metro bus hub — the only site in the region with the scale and intermodal connectivity required for a true transportation hub. Structural assessment complete; $2.15M stabilization scope identified. Federal Historic Tax Credits (20% of qualified expenses) reduce effective renovation cost.

The Regulatory Moment

On March 9, 2026, the U.S. Department of Transportation announced 8 national eIPP (eVTOL Integration Pilot Program) sites — including Texas DOT + Archer Aviation, with named routes in Dallas, Austin, San Antonio, and Houston. The eIPP enables revenue passenger operations before full FAA type certification, beginning H2 2026. Fort Worth is not yet named — making immediate positioning as the Fort Worth vertiport anchor critical.
