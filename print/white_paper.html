Executive Overview

The Trinity Air Link Transportation System proposes the adaptive reuse of the historic Texas & Pacific Warehouse in downtown Fort Worth into the nation's first fully integrated multi-modal mobility hub — combining electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) aircraft, Level 4 autonomous electric vehicles, a 120,000 square foot technology innovation center, and AI-powered systems management into a single, seamlessly connected infrastructure platform.

This is not a speculative concept. The technologies that power the Link are commercially operational today. Waymo delivers 450,000+ paid autonomous rides per week across five U.S. cities including Austin, Texas. Archer Aviation holds FAA Part 135 Air Carrier, Part 145 Repair Station, and Part 141 Pilot Training certificates and begins Texas air taxi operations under the federal eIPP program in H2 2026. Joby Aviation completed FAA Stage 4 of 5 type certification in November 2025.

Section 4

The Solution: Trinity Air Link

Trinity Air Link — integrating historic architecture with next-generation mobility.

The Trinity Air Link integrates four functional systems within the historic Texas & Pacific Warehouse:

1. eVTOL Air Mobility Terminal

Rooftop vertiport with multiple simultaneous landing pads, rapid charging systems, passenger boarding facilities, and operations center. Initial 10–15 aircraft fleet scaling to 25–40 by Year 5.

2. Autonomous Vehicle Ground Terminal

Ground-level Level 4 AV fleet providing first- and last-mile connectivity across the Fort Worth urban core, connecting to neighborhoods, business districts, Texas A&M campus, and TEXRail commuter rail.

3. Technology Innovation Center

120,000 sq ft of leasable R&D space on floors 2–7 for transportation technology companies, academic partners, battery developers, and AI research programs at Fort Worth Class A rates of $29–$37/sq ft.

4. AI-Powered Systems Integration

Proprietary central command platform managing real-time scheduling, predictive maintenance, weather-adaptive routing, and seamless passenger handoff between VTOL and AV services — all via a single consumer app.

Section 5

The Asset: Texas & Pacific Warehouse

The Texas & Pacific Warehouse — 8 stories, 600 ft × 100 ft, downtown Fort Worth.

Built in the 1920s for the Texas & Pacific Railway, the T&P Warehouse is an 8-story, 600×100-foot masonry and concrete structure — approximately 480,000 total sq ft — located at the convergence of downtown Fort Worth, the Panther Island development, and TEXRail commuter rail.

Vacant since 1998. A structural assessment conducted in 2023 identified $2.15M in stabilization work. The structural core is sound; the stabilization scope is defined and budgeted. The Link acquires this landmark without displacement or demolition costs.

Strategic Advantages

  • Rooftop large enough for simultaneous VTOL operations
  • Floor plates designed for heavy industrial loads
  • Basement and upper floors for computing infrastructure
  • Direct TEXRail commuter rail connection
  • Historic Tax Credit eligibility (20% of qualified expenses)
  • 27-year vacancy = no displacement costs
  • City-motivated seller; structural scope defined
Historic Significance: The T&P Warehouse was built to move people by rail. The Link returns it to that purpose — at 150 mph, above the rooftops of a city growing faster than its roads can carry it.

Section 8

Global Precedents

🇸🇬 Singapore — Regulatory-First AV

Dedicated AV test zones with structured government support and phased regulatory development produced the fastest commercial AV deployment timeline of any major city. Lesson: regulatory relationships built early determine deployment speed.

🇦🇪 Dubai — UAM Infrastructure Before Operators

Dubai's Roads and Transport Authority committed to vertiport investment before commercial eVTOL certification was complete — securing first-mover advantage in airspace management and passenger experience. Lesson: infrastructure investment that precedes operators sets the partnership terms.

🇰🇷 Seoul — AI Traffic Management at Scale

Seoul's integrated digital twin and AI traffic management platform demonstrated a 20%+ reduction in urban congestion on managed corridors. The Link's AI integration layer is modeled on this approach. Lesson: data integration at the systems level compounds over time.

🇬🇧 London — Historic Station as Mobility Hub

King's Cross and Paddington conversions demonstrate that adaptive reuse of historic transportation buildings creates iconic civic anchors that increase both ridership and surrounding property values. Lesson: historic character amplifies commercial appeal.

🇺🇸 Los Angeles — Union Station AV Integration

LA Metro's Union Station mobility hub — integrating rail, bus, AV testing, and adjacent mixed-use development — is the closest U.S. analog to the Link concept. Its public-private partnership structure provides a direct template. Lesson: PPP structures align municipal and private incentives across a decade-long horizon.
