





 







"The Future Begins at T&P"
In the 1930s, the Texas and Pacific Warehouse revolutionized transportation in Fort Worth as part of a $13 million investment in rail infrastructure that helped build the modern DFW metroplex. Today, we stand at the threshold of an even greater transportation revolution - one that will fundamentally reshape how we move, work, and live. The Link project envisions transforming this historic building into the epicenter of next-generation mobility and technology innovation for the DFW area.
The Vision: By reimagining the T&P Warehouse as a central node in an advanced transportation network, we will create a first-of-its-kind integration of:
•	Vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) aircraft operations
•	Autonomous electric vehicle networks
•	Advanced robotics and artificial intelligence research facilities
•	Technology innovation spaces and public showcases
•	High-performance computing infrastructure
Market Opportunity: The DFW metroplex, with its population of 7.4 million and robust economic growth, is ideally positioned to pioneer this transportation revolution. The T&P Warehouse sits at the convergence of over $500 million in planned development, including Texas A&M's new $320 million campus and the $217 million Omni Hotel expansion. This location, combined with existing rail infrastructure and downtown accessibility, creates a unique opportunity to build the future of urban mobility.
Community Impact: The Link project will:
•	Create thousands of high-tech jobs
•	Establish DFW as a leader in transportation innovation
•	Preserve and revitalize a historic landmark
•	Reduce traffic congestion and emissions
•	Attract technology companies and investment
•	Generate significant economic growth

Historical Context & Future Vision
A Legacy of Transportation Innovation In the 1930s, the Texas and Pacific Railway Company made a bold investment that would shape Fort Worth's future. The T&P Warehouse, part of a visionary three-building complex, represented the pinnacle of transportation technology for its era. As rail gave way to air travel in the 1950s, this architectural landmark entered a period of dormancy - but its potential to revolutionize transportation never diminished.
Today's Technological Convergence We stand at a similar inflection point in transportation history. Multiple transformative technologies are maturing simultaneously:
•	Electric vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) aircraft making urban air mobility possible
•	Autonomous vehicles reshaping ground transportation
•	Artificial intelligence and robotics transforming logistics
•	Virtual and augmented reality changing how we experience travel
•	Advanced computing enabling real-time transportation management
The Trinity Air Link Vision Just as this building once served as a crucial hub for rail freight, connecting Fort Worth to the nation's rail network, we envision it becoming the nexus of future transportation technologies. The building's original design - with its robust structure, strategic location, and expansive spaces - makes it uniquely suited for this transformation.
Key elements of the transformation include:
•	Converting the rooftop for VTOL operations
•	Utilizing the ground floor for autonomous vehicle operations
•	Transforming interior spaces into technology research and development centers
•	Creating public spaces that showcase emerging technologies
•	Establishing computing infrastructure to manage next-generation transportation networks
Bridging Past and Future This vision honors the building's heritage while propelling it into the future. The same spirit of innovation that drove the original T&P development will guide its transformation into a hub for future mobility. By preserving this historic structure while adapting it for tomorrow's transportation needs, we create a living bridge between Fort Worth's past and its future.

The Link System
Network Overview The Link system reimagines urban mobility through a seamlessly integrated network of air and ground transportation. With the T&P Warehouse as its central node, this system will connect the DFW metroplex through:
•	A network of VTOL aircraft providing rapid city-to-city transport
•	Autonomous electric vehicles offering efficient ground transportation
•	Smart traffic management systems optimizing route efficiency
•	Integration with existing rail and public transit infrastructure
Central Node: Trinity Air Link The T&P Warehouse will serve as the flagship Trinity Air Link hub, showcasing the full potential of integrated transportation:
Ground Level Operations:
•	Autonomous vehicle docking and charging stations
•	Passenger terminal with seamless modal transfers
•	Smart baggage and cargo handling systems
•	Direct connection to existing rail infrastructure
Rooftop VTOL Port:
•	Multiple VTOL landing pads
•	Advanced air traffic management systems
•	Weather monitoring and safety systems
•	Passenger boarding and waiting areas
Technology Integration The Link system leverages cutting-edge technologies to ensure safe, efficient operations:
•	AI-powered traffic management
•	Real-time route optimization
•	Predictive maintenance systems
•	Integrated passenger booking and tracking
•	Advanced security systems
Network Expansion From the central Trinity Air Link hub, the network will expand through strategic placement of secondary hubs across the DFW metroplex:
•	Major business districts
•	Residential centers
•	Entertainment venues
•	Educational institutions
•	Healthcare facilities
This hub-and-spoke model allows for scalable growth while maintaining efficient operations and maximizing coverage of the metropolitan area.
